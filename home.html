A captivating mixture of the old and new, Brechin, in Angus, is a blend of tradition and modernity. In ancient times it was called a city because of the cathedral and its role as the center of a Roman Catholic jurisdiction.

Brechin's Cathedral is famous for being the only one in Angus. It is also home to an extraordinary round tower from the 11th century which happens to be one of Scotland’s two remaining towers that stand out among others. In Brechin, tourists can learn about its past by visiting attractions such as Caledonian Railway with its steam and diesel engines.

The Townhouse Museum situated in an 18th-century building narrates this history further. For outdoor lovers there exists relaxation in 70-acre country park known as Brechin Castle Centre with amenities like a playground, an enchanting fairy trail, huge garden centre and welcoming restaurant.

The visitors will have many memories from touring historical sites to engaging themselves in cultural activities or just enjoying nature’s peace at Brechin.
